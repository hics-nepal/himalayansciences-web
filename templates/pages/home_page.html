IESH Environmental Monitor
Continuous atmospheric monitoring from Kathmandu. Temperature, humidity, pressure, air quality — syncing live to himalayansciences.org. First in a network spanning Nepal's altitude gradient, from the Terai lowlands to high Himalayan stations.

Crystal Mountain School, Dolpo
Thermal performance monitoring during construction of a passive solar multipurpose hall at 4,000m in Dho Tarap. The first empirical thermal dataset for passive solar construction at this altitude in Nepal.

→ Building physics programme

◎ In progress
Cosmic Ray Muon Detector
Nepal's first locally manufactured cosmic ray muon detector, based on the CosmicWatch v3X open-source architecture. Field measurements from the Terai (~150m) to Himalayan passes above 5,000m — enabling environmental monitoring, glacier research, and disaster risk reduction.

→ Muon detector

The gap in the data

Nepal sits at the intersection of some of the most important scientific questions of our time — atmospheric pollution, seismic hazard, climate adaptation, cosmic ray physics. It is also one of the most under-instrumented countries on Earth.

HICS is building the measurement infrastructure to change this.

Atmospheric monitoring
Nepal's atmosphere across its full altitude gradient — from the Terai to the high Himalaya — is almost entirely unmeasured. Transboundary pollution from the Indo-Gangetic Plain reaches the mountains — there is almost no data on what arrives, or how it changes with altitude.
Cosmic ray flux
Nepal's altitude gradient — from 60m to 8,848m — is the most dramatic on Earth. It has never been systematically measured for cosmic ray muon flux. HICS's muon detector will generate this dataset.
Building performance
Passive solar design for Himalayan conditions requires local empirical data. None exists at high altitude in Nepal. The Crystal Mountain School dataset is the first.

Education & open knowledge

HICS's education programmes bring instruments, data, and scientific practice directly into schools and communities. Not textbook science — students analyse real data from real instruments collecting real signals.

School Science Residency
Multi-day residencies combining planetarium sessions, instrument workshops, data analysis, and teacher training. Schools enter lasting relationships, not one-off events.

→ Details

Youth Research Fellowship
Full-time 12–24 month research positions for Nepali scientists. Co-authored publications. An explicit alternative to emigrating to do science.

→ Details

Annual Science Camp
Ten-day residential programme for high school students selected from across Nepal, with priority for students from districts outside Kathmandu.

→ Details

Teacher Professional Development
Residential intensive. Teachers leave with a working instrument they built, curriculum modules ready to teach, and membership in an ongoing professional community.

→ Details

Gender equity is structural, not symbolic: fellowship selection, camp admissions, teacher recruitment, and mentoring structures all explicitly work to reduce barriers for women and girls.
